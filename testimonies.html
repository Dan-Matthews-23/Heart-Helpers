Mary's story

"I was born with a heart defect which means my heartbeat is a little different to everyone else's. Sometimes my heart just isn't enough on its own to get blood around my body. For that, I need a little extra help. I have a small device - called an ICD - attached to my heart. It sends an electric shock to my heart if it starts beating irregularly, and without this device, I could die. I need to have regular appointments with a consultant to make sure it's working properly. I need to be careful not to move too much in case the leads move, and there's always the chance that the ICD might stop working as it should. With your help, we can fund vital research into cause, treatment and cure of all heart disease. It really is so important we do everything we can."

Brian's story

"Fifteen years ago I suffered a major heart attack. I reached out to Heart Helpers and I've never regeretted it. They put me in touch with a support worker who I still talk to now, and would even go so far as to call them a good friend. I'm not sure where I would have been without Heart Helpers. That's why I always help out where I can. I can't get out and about as much as I once could, but it doesn't matter - there are loads of things you can do to help. So please, if you're not sure, get in touch with Heart Helpers. You won't regret it!"
